# PoloPro Operator Landing Redesign

## Goal

Turn PoloPro landing page into a dependable self-serve conversion page for photo booth operators.

## Audience and primary action

- Audience: photo booth operators running live events.
- Primary action: create an account through the existing signup route.
- Secondary action: download booth apps after account intent is established.

## Visual direction

Use an operator-control-room identity: graphite surfaces, crisp white copy, electric mint for active and successful states, and restrained coral for live-event urgency. Product screenshots remain evidence, not decoration. A subtle light-trace frame around visuals is the page signature.

## Page structure

1. Fixed header: persistent `Start free` action; navigation remains reachable on small screens.
2. Hero: operational headline, short outcome-led explanation, signup-first CTA, app-download secondary CTA, and concise trust proof.
3. Workflow: four chronological live-event stages—create, prepare, run, deliver.
4. Product proof: dashboard, templates, booth app, and guest gallery sections use screenshots, capability lists, and direct operator benefits.
5. Capability grid and app downloads: consistent cards with clear labels and target actions.
6. Footer: keep privacy and support contact paths.

## UX and accessibility requirements

- Keep CTA vocabulary consistent: `Start free` creates an account; `Download apps` scrolls to installers.
- Preserve existing routes, downloads, app-version loading, and all product claims.
- Use visible keyboard focus, adequate contrast, reduced-motion fallback, responsive layouts, and touch-sized actions.
- Do not add dependencies or change backend behavior.

## Verification

- Confirm HTML parses and all local asset references resolve.
- Review desktop and mobile screenshots for hierarchy, overflow, CTA visibility, and screenshot legibility.
